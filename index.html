About me

Hi! I am a PhD student within the Center for Doctoral training in Next Generation Computational Modelling (CTD-NGCM) at the University of Southampton. I am part of the Maritime Engineering Group and I am based in the Bolderwood campus. I am a first year PhD student with Associate Professor Gabriel Weymouth and Associate Professor Georges Limbert as my supervisory team.

I earned a Bachelor of Engineering (First Class) in Yacht and Powercraft Design where my dissertation was awarded the (2017 RINA – BAE Systems Student Naval Architect Award - Final Year Project). I then pursued a Master of Science (First Class) in Computationnal Fluid Dynamics where I focused on the Numerical modelling of ship manoeuvring using Computational Fluid Dynamics.

Interests

I’m interested in various different field of Computational fluid mechanics, from fluid-structure interaction and the complex mechanics involved to classical turbulence problem. My research is focus of modelling the interplay between flexible membranes and an unsteady flow, for this I am coupling a Finite-Volume Cartesian grid fluid solver and a Finite-Element solid mechanic solver. These has application in a wide range of flieds, from modelling of foliage, parachutes, sails, heart valves and insect wings. Outside of Computationnal fluid dynamics, I have intersts in sailing yacht performance prediction, weather forecasting, nunerical methods (applied to the aforemmentionned problems) and a bit a non-linear systems/chaos theory.

When I am note doing one of the above, I can usually be found doing some of my favorite activities i.e sailing, kitesurfing or cycling.
